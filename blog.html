The first step to helping your young one is to understand the problem

Mental Health

This is defined as health conditions involving changes in emotion, thinking or behavior (or a combination of these). There are a wide range of disorders, some of the most common being: Depression, Anxiety, Bipolar, OCD, Schizophrenia.
Mental illnesses can become present at any age

Cyber Bullying

Throughout the year’s social media has blown up in ways that have been linked to causing depression and anxiety due to bullying, showcasing issues on body images, and making others feel left out. Depression is the leading factor in adolescents caused by social media because of cyber bullying, body image issues, stalking, and peers

Dormant Illnesses

The physical environment of a person can also trigger dormant mental health issues or create new ones, especially with adolescents since their brains and bodies are still developing. Multiple outside sources can be the cause, outdoor physical environments, housing, or people and relationships. When the brain is still developing it is easily influenced by the people around it. If a young person is constantly being ridiculed, bullied, or singled out by their peers they will most likely start to develop low self-esteem and other mental issues.

How can you help?

The most important things you can do is...

Support them!

A child's healthy development relies on their parents because they are the first source of support and care. Kids need to feel loved, nurtured, and accepted; they need to have the security that they can count on their parents when in tough situations. They need someone to rely on because kids don’t quite know yet how to defend themselves or know how to practice healthy coping mechanisms.
When a parent is present but absent emotionally “The child will feel rejected regularly” (How to avoid the trap of present but absent parenting). The parenting technique to let a baby “cry it out” the child handle the situation by themself. This method of parenting is harmful and leaves the child feeling neglected, “those whose needs go unmet will become mistrustful and struggle with feelings of insecurity and worthlessness”(How an emotionally absent mother impacts her daughter's life).
Many parents believe that bullying or ridicule from their peers is a part of growing up, but it only causes harm when the parents don't get step in to protect their child. It’s almost impossible for a young child to healthily develop and learn how to properly deal with mental issues because their emotions were ignored at such a young age.

Pay attention!

Many parents don’t monitor their children or are simply unaware of the dangers of the media. So when kids fall into these holes they are often unprotected from the harsh reality of life and people. They can be exposed to cyber bullying, as discussed previously, and without protection from their parents they are stuck fending for themselves.
Start by noticing the moods, emotions, and behaviors. Early harm intervention, especially with signs of it, is critical because mental health conditions often worsen without treatment. If noticing symptoms, schedule an appointment with a licensed psychiatrist as soon as possible, or if that is not possible, then the pediatrician or primary care physician. Make sure that healthcare professionals are given as much detailed information.
If the child reports seeing or hearing things that are not there, without the influence of drugs or alcohol, then seek medical treatment immediately. With that being said, continue helping them by educating yourself as much as possible about their mental health condition. One website that can benefit the child when educating yourself is NAMI Basics. NAMI Basics is an educational class that teaches parents and other family caregivers how to cope with their child’s condition and manage their recovery.
